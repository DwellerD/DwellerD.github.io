About Me

IT Background Meets Software QA

I started in IT support and asset coordination, where I learned how important reliability, documentation, organization, and clear communication are when people depend on systems working correctly. That experience gave me a practical foundation for troubleshooting issues, following processes, supporting users, and thinking carefully about how work moves through a system.

My Epic TDR testing experience pushed me further toward Software QA. It helped me practice validating workflows, executing functional tests, documenting defects, and looking at software from both a user perspective and an operational perspective.

Today, I am focused on growing into a QA Engineer or automation focused testing role. My portfolio is built around personal software projects where I can plan features, test workflows, validate database rules, document risks, and create practical QA artifacts. Outside of software, I like playing guitar and piano, staying active, and building creative projects that help me keep learning by doing.
